James (he/him) is a spatial ecologist who now works as a GIS and Data Systems Specialist at Trent University. I have completed many large projects in remote sensing of semi-aquatic habitats using [planes](https://macsphere.mcmaster.ca/bitstreams/4686ccb0-e53b-47b2-b4ef-29ec369809cc/download), [train(ed drone)s](https://cdnsciencepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1139/juvs-2015-0016), and [satellites?](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/07038992.2021.1946385) 
(Unfortunately I couldn't complete the rhyming set, though I did look at automobile roads in the first example as well as [here](https://prod-ms-be.lib.mcmaster.ca/server/api/core/bitstreams/58d3ae64-9c3b-422f-ae7b-6bacf174a67a/content). My work now focusses on supporting research projects through back-end tech, workshops, and student help. 

I earned my Ph.D. in Biology from McMaster University in the Chow-Fraser Lab and mostly focussed on [invasive *Phragmites*](https://macsphere.mcmaster.ca/bitstream/11375/24346/2/Marcaccio_James_V_2019April_PhD.pdf). Following that, I worked at Fisheries & Oceans Canada (aka DFO) in the Midwood Lab completing projects on remote sensing of fish habitat. Some [worked](https://waves-vagues.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/library-bibliotheque/41120796.pdf), some [didn't](https://waves-vagues.dfo-mpo.gc.ca/library-bibliotheque/41291116.pdf), but that's how science goes!
